# Enneagram Type Descriptions

## Type 1: The Reformer (The Perfectionist)
**Core Motivation:** To be right, good, and to improve everything
**Core Fear:** Being wrong, bad, evil, or corrupt
**Core Desire:** To be good, balanced, have integrity
**Key Traits:** Principled, purposeful, self-controlled, perfectionistic, critical (of self and others), organized, ethical, detail-oriented
**At Their Best:** Wise, discerning, realistic, and noble with a strong sense of right and wrong
**Under Stress:** Become moody and irrational (move to unhealthy 4)
**In Growth:** Become more spontaneous and joyful (move to healthy 7)
**Wing Variants:** 1w9 (more idealistic, calm) or 1w2 (more warm, helpful)

## Type 2: The Helper (The Giver)
**Core Motivation:** To be loved and needed by others
**Core Fear:** Being unwanted, unworthy of love
**Core Desire:** To feel loved and appreciated
**Key Traits:** Generous, demonstrative, people-pleasing, possessive, empathetic, warm, nurturing, can be manipulative
**At Their Best:** Unselfish, altruistic, genuinely caring for others
**Under Stress:** Become aggressive and dominating (move to unhealthy 8)
**In Growth:** Become more self-nurturing (move to healthy 4)
**Wing Variants:** 2w1 (more idealistic, critical) or 2w3 (more ambitious, image-conscious)

## Type 3: The Achiever (The Performer)
**Core Motivation:** To be successful and admired
**Core Fear:** Being worthless, failing
**Core Desire:** To feel valuable and worthwhile
**Key Traits:** Adaptable, excelling, driven, image-conscious, competitive, efficient, concerned with status
**At Their Best:** Self-accepting, authentic, inspiring role models
**Under Stress:** Become disengaged and apathetic (move to unhealthy 9)
**In Growth:** Become more cooperative and committed (move to healthy 6)
**Wing Variants:** 3w2 (more charming, social) or 3w4 (more introspective, artistic)

## Type 4: The Individualist (The Romantic)
**Core Motivation:** To be unique, authentic, and express themselves
**Core Fear:** Being ordinary, flawed, or emotionally cut off
**Core Desire:** To find themselves and their significance
**Key Traits:** Expressive, dramatic, self-absorbed, temperamental, creative, sensitive, moody, envious of others
**At Their Best:** Inspired, creative, able to transform experiences with self-awareness
**Under Stress:** Become over-involved and clingy (move to unhealthy 2)
**In Growth:** Become more objective and principled (move to healthy 1)
**Wing Variants:** 4w3 (more ambitious, extroverted) or 4w5 (more withdrawn, intellectual)

## Type 5: The Investigator (The Observer)
**Core Motivation:** To understand the world, to be capable and competent
**Core Fear:** Being useless, helpless, incompetent
**Core Desire:** To be knowledgeable and capable
**Key Traits:** Perceptive, innovative, secretive, isolated, cerebral, curious, detached, can hoard knowledge/resources
**At Their Best:** Pioneering, groundbreaking visionaries
**Under Stress:** Become hyperactive and scattered (move to unhealthy 7)
**In Growth:** Become more confident and decisive (move to healthy 8)
**Wing Variants:** 5w4 (more creative, iconoclastic) or 5w6 (more loyal, security-oriented)

## Type 6: The Loyalist (The Skeptic)
**Core Motivation:** To have security and support
**Core Fear:** Being without support, guidance, or security
**Core Desire:** To have security and support
**Key Traits:** Engaging, responsible, anxious, suspicious, hardworking, loyal, worst-case scenario thinking, seek authority/rebel against it
**At Their Best:** Courageous, self-reliant, committed, trust themselves
**Under Stress:** Become competitive and arrogant (move to unhealthy 3)
**In Growth:** Become more relaxed and optimistic (move to healthy 9)
**Wing Variants:** 6w5 (more intellectual, cautious) or 6w7 (more social, engaging)

## Type 7: The Enthusiast (The Epicure)
**Core Motivation:** To be satisfied, content, and experience life fully
**Core Fear:** Being deprived, trapped in pain
**Core Desire:** To be happy, satisfied, and fulfilled
**Key Traits:** Spontaneous, versatile, acquisitive, scattered, optimistic, excitable, can be impulsive, avoid pain
**At Their Best:** Joyous, highly accomplished, focused, and grateful
**Under Stress:** Become perfectionistic and critical (move to unhealthy 1)
**In Growth:** Become more focused and sober (move to healthy 5)
**Wing Variants:** 7w6 (more responsible, loyal) or 7w8 (more assertive, materialistic)

## Type 8: The Challenger (The Protector)
**Core Motivation:** To be strong and protect themselves from vulnerability
**Core Fear:** Being harmed, controlled, or violated
**Core Desire:** To protect themselves and determine their own course
**Key Traits:** Self-confident, decisive, willful, confrontational, powerful, dominating, protective, direct, can be aggressive
**At Their Best:** Heroic, magnanimous, use strength for good
**Under Stress:** Become secretive and withdrawn (move to unhealthy 5)
**In Growth:** Become more open-hearted and caring (move to healthy 2)
**Wing Variants:** 8w7 (more outgoing, energetic) or 8w9 (more calm, approachable)

## Type 9: The Peacemaker (The Mediator)
**Core Motivation:** To have inner stability and peace of mind
**Core Fear:** Loss, separation, fragmentation
**Core Desire:** To have peace of mind and harmony
**Key Traits:** Receptive, reassuring, complacent, easygoing, supportive, agreeable, merge with others' agendas, avoid conflict
**At Their Best:** Deeply accepting, authentic, truly peaceful
**Under Stress:** Become anxious and worried (move to unhealthy 6)
**In Growth:** Become more energetic and engaged (move to healthy 3)
**Wing Variants:** 9w8 (more assertive, confident) or 9w1 (more idealistic, critical)

## Centers of Intelligence

**Gut Center (8, 9, 1):** React to the world through instinct, anger is the underlying emotion
**Heart Center (2, 3, 4):** React through feelings, shame is the underlying emotion
**Head Center (5, 6, 7):** React through thinking, fear is the underlying emotion
